📊 System Sizing Guide for Bangladesh Homes

Properly sizing your solar system is crucial for maximizing returns. Below is a comprehensive guide to help you determine the ideal system size based on your electricity consumption and budget.

💡 How to Calculate Your Needs

  1. 1. Check your monthly electricity bills for average usage
  2. 2. Multiply kWh by 1.2 to account for system losses
  3. 3. Divide by 30 for daily consumption
  4. 4. Divide by local solar hours (4.5-5.0 in Bangladesh)
  5. 5. Result = Minimum kW capacity needed
  6. Example: 300kWh/month ÷ 30 days = 10kWh/day ÷ 4.8 hours = 2.08kW system

🏠 Typical System Recommendations

  • Small Apartment (1-2 rooms): 1-2 kW system
  • Medium House (2-3 bedrooms): 3-4 kW system
  • Large House (4+ bedrooms): 5-10 kW system
  • With AC Units: Add 1.5 kW per ton of AC capacity
  • With Electric Water Heaters: Add 1 kW
  • Net Zero Energy: Size based on 120% of annual consumption

⚡ Net Metering in Bangladesh

Bangladesh's net metering policy, established by SREDA and BPDB, allows solar system owners to feed excess electricity back to the grid, offsetting their consumption and significantly improving system ROI.

Eligibility 3-phase connections standard; single-phase in pilot areas Wide accessibility for most homes Valid electricity connection

System Size Limits Up to 100% of sanctioned load Can offset entire electricity bill SREDA-approved system design

📋 Net Metering Application Process

  1. 1. Technical Feasibility Study: Contact local BPDB office for initial assessment (7-10 days)
  2. 2. Submit Application: Complete Form A with system details and ownership proof (1 day)
  3. 3. Technical Evaluation: BPDB engineers evaluate connection feasibility (14-21 days)
  4. 4. Connection Agreement: Sign net metering agreement with payment of fees (3-5 days)
  5. 5. System Installation: Install solar system per approved specifications (varies)
  6. 6. Testing & Commissioning: BPDB inspection and approval (7-10 days)
  7. 7. Meter Installation: Bi-directional meter installation by BPDB (3-5 days)
  8. 8. Final Connection: System connected to grid with net metering activated (1-2 days)

Total timeline: 35-55 days from application to activation

🔌 Grid-Tie vs. Battery Systems

Given Bangladesh's frequent load shedding but improving net metering policy, choosing between grid-tie and battery systems requires careful consideration of your specific needs and budget.

⚡ Grid-Tie System

Pros:

  • • Lower initial cost (30-40% cheaper)
  • • Higher ROI (5-7 year payback)
  • • Simpler installation and maintenance
  • • No battery replacement costs
  • • Grid acts as unlimited storage

Cons:

  • • No power during grid outages
  • • Dependent on net metering policy
  • • Cannot store excess production

Best For:

  • • Areas with reliable grid power
  • • Budget-conscious homeowners
  • • Maximum financial returns
  • • Areas with stable net metering

🔋 Hybrid System

Pros:

  • • Best of both worlds
  • • Power during grid outages
  • • Net metering benefits
  • • Can prioritize self-consumption
  • • Expandable capacity

Cons:

  • • Higher cost than grid-tie
  • • More complex installation
  • • Battery maintenance required

Best For:

  • • Areas with occasional load shedding
  • • Those needing backup for essential loads
  • • Future-proofing against policy changes

🔋 Battery-Backup System

Pros:

  • • Complete energy independence
  • • Uninterrupted power supply
  • • Protection against tariff increases
  • • Store excess production
  • • No grid connection required

Cons:

  • • Highest initial investment
  • • Battery replacement costs (7-10 years)
  • • Longer payback period (8-12 years)
  • • Requires more maintenance

Best For:

  • • Areas with frequent load shedding
  • • Remote locations with poor grid reliability
  • • Those prioritizing energy independence
  • • Critical power needs (medical equipment, etc.)

🔧 Complete Installation Process

📋 Step 1: Site Assessment & Planning

  • Roof Structural Analysis: Ensure roof can support 15-20kg/m² additional load
  • Solar Access Study: Measure sunlight hours, identify shading issues
  • Electrical System Inspection: Verify panel capacity, earthing quality
  • Space Planning: 8-10 m² roof space needed per kW capacity
  • Orientation & Tilt: South-facing with 10-15° tilt optimal for Bangladesh
  • Timeline: 3-5 days for thorough assessment

📝 Step 2: Permits & Documentation

  • SREDA Registration: Submit Form-A for renewable energy installation
  • BPDB Application: For net metering connection approval
  • Local Authority Clearance: Building permission if structure modified
  • Insurance Documentation: Update home insurance to cover solar system
  • Timeline: 14-21 days for approvals

🛠️ Step 3: System Installation

  • Mounting Structure: Rust-resistant aluminum rails with weather sealing
  • Panel Installation: Secure mounting with proper spacing for ventilation
  • Wiring & Junction Boxes: UV-resistant solar cables, proper conduits
  • Inverter Mounting: In shaded, ventilated area, protected from rain
  • Battery Setup (if applicable): In ventilated, temperature-controlled space
  • Electrical Connections: DC/AC wiring, grounding, surge protection
  • Timeline: 2-5 days depending on system size

✅ Step 4: Testing & Commissioning

  • Electrical Safety Tests: Insulation, grounding, polarity verification
  • Performance Validation: Power output, efficiency testing
  • Meter Installation: Bi-directional meter by BPDB for net metering
  • System Handover: Documentation, warranty certificates, user training
  • Timeline: 1-2 days for testing, 3-7 days for utility connection

🔍 Maintenance & System Longevity

🧹 Routine Maintenance

  • Panel Cleaning: Every 2-4 weeks during dry season, monthly during monsoon
  • Connection Checks: Quarterly inspection for loose connections
  • Inverter Inspection: Bi-annual check of error logs and ventilation
  • System Monitoring: Weekly performance review via app/monitoring
  • Battery Maintenance: Monthly water level and terminal checks for lead-acid; minimal for lithium
  • Vegetation Control: Ensure no shading from growing plants
  • Annual Professional Inspection: Comprehensive system check by certified technician

⚠️ Common Issues in Bangladesh

  • Monsoon Challenges: Water ingress in junction boxes, check waterproofing
  • Cyclone Preparation: Additional mounting reinforcement in coastal areas
  • Dust Buildup: Efficiency loss of 15-25% without regular cleaning
  • Heat Degradation: Ensure proper panel ventilation in hot months
  • Corrosion: Salt exposure in coastal areas requires marine-grade components
  • Bird Nesting: Install bird deterrents to prevent nests under panels
  • Grid Voltage Fluctuation: Quality inverters with wide operating range essential

⏳ Component Lifespans

  • Solar Panels: 25-30 years (0.5-0.7% annual degradation)
  • String Inverters: 10-15 years (replace once in system lifetime)
  • Microinverters: 15-25 years (longer lifespan, higher initial cost)
  • Lithium Batteries: 7-10 years (2,000-3,000 cycles)
  • Lead-Acid Batteries: 3-5 years (500-1,000 cycles)
  • Mounting Hardware: 25+ years with proper installation
  • Charge Controllers: 10-15 years for quality units
  • DC/AC Disconnects: 15-25 years with minimal maintenance

📈 Performance Monitoring

  • Mobile Apps: Most inverters offer free monitoring apps

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How much roof space do I need for a solar system in Bangladesh?

A: In Bangladesh, you'll need approximately 8-10 square meters of unshaded roof space per kW of solar capacity. A typical 3kW system requires 24-30 square meters. South-facing roofs with a 10-15° tilt are ideal, but east/west orientations can still achieve 85-90% efficiency. If roof space is limited, consider higher-efficiency monocrystalline panels.

Q: Can I install solar panels during monsoon season in Bangladesh?

A: Installation during monsoon season (June-September) is possible but not recommended. Rain and wind create safety hazards for installers, and roof work may damage waterproofing. Additionally, proper sealing and weatherproofing is more difficult in wet conditions. The ideal installation window is October-March during dry season. If you must install during monsoon, ensure the installer has proper safety protocols and plans for weather breaks.

Q: How do I know if my home qualifies for net metering in Bangladesh?

A: To qualify for net metering in Bangladesh, your home must have: 1) A legal grid connection with no outstanding bills, 2) Sufficient roof space for solar installation, 3) Three-phase connection in most areas (though single-phase pilots exist in some regions), 4) No significant historical electricity theft or tampering, and 5) A local BPDB office that's implementing the program (most urban areas). Contact your local BPDB office for a preliminary assessment.

Q: Do solar panels work during load shedding?

A: Standard grid-tie solar systems automatically shut down during load shedding for safety reasons. However, hybrid systems with batteries or special inverters can continue powering your home during outages. For Bangladesh's load shedding conditions, consider: 1) A hybrid inverter with battery backup, 2) A grid-tie inverter with Uninterrupted Power Supply (UPS) functionality, or 3) A grid-tie system with manual transfer switch to an existing backup system. These solutions add 30-60% to system cost but provide power continuity.

Q: Which solar panels work best in Bangladesh's hot climate?

A: For Bangladesh's hot and humid climate, Tier-1 monocrystalline PERC panels with low temperature coefficient (below -0.38%/°C) perform best. Look for panels with salt mist and ammonia corrosion certification (IEC 61701 & IEC 62716) for coastal areas. Half-cut cell technology reduces heat-related losses, while glass-glass construction better withstands humidity. Recommended brands include Longi, JA Solar, Jinko, Canadian Solar, and REC—all with proven performance in Bangladesh's climate conditions.

Q: How do I clean solar panels during dusty season?

A: Clean panels during early morning or evening when they're cool. Use a soft brush or microfiber cloth with distilled water and mild soap (never abrasive cleaners). For roof safety, use extension tools or hire professionals. During December-February dust season, clean every 2-3 weeks; during other months, monthly cleaning is sufficient. Avoid metal tools that can scratch glass surfaces. In urban areas with heavy pollution, consider professional cleaning services twice yearly for thorough removal of stubborn deposits.
