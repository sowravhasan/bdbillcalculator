Load shedding remains a persistent challenge across Bangladesh, with many areas experiencing 2-8 hours of daily outages. Whether you're in Dhaka, Chattogram, Rajshahi, or rural areas, power cuts disrupt productivity, comfort, and even essential services. This comprehensive guide provides practical, affordable solutions to minimize the impact of load shedding on your home or business in 2025.

Success Story: The Rahman family in Mirpur implemented our tiered backup strategy and reduced their power outage disruption time by 85%, while saving Tk3,500 monthly on emergency power solutions.

📊 Understanding Bangladesh Load Shedding in 2025

Before implementing solutions, it's essential to understand the current load shedding patterns in Bangladesh:

Current Load Shedding Statistics (September 2025):

Areas by Severity:

  • High (5-8 hours/day): Khulna, Sylhet, Rural areas
  • Medium (3-5 hours/day): Parts of Dhaka, Chattogram, Rajshahi
  • Low (1-3 hours/day): Gulshan, Banani, Urban centers

Pattern Analysis:

  • Peak hours: 11 AM-2 PM, 6 PM-10 PM
  • Seasonal peaks: June-September (summer), March-April (pre-monsoon)
  • Predictability: 60% follow scheduled times, 40% unscheduled

Despite government efforts to increase power generation capacity, load shedding remains a daily reality for most Bangladeshis due to infrastructure challenges, increasing demand, and fuel shortages. The good news is that with proper planning, you can minimize its impact on your daily life.

💡 Pro Tip: Create Your Own Prediction System

Even when official schedules aren't reliable, you can create your own prediction system:

  1. Keep a 2-week log of power outage times
  2. Identify patterns by day of week and time of day
  3. Create a basic schedule with "high probability" outage windows
  4. Set phone alerts 30 minutes before likely outages
  5. Update your predictions weekly as patterns change

⚡ Comprehensive Backup Power Solutions

The 4-Tier Backup Strategy

We recommend a tiered approach to backup power based on your budget and needs:

1

Essential Tier: Battery-Powered Solutions

Budget-friendly options that everyone should have:

  • • Emergency LED lights (Tk300-600)
  • • Rechargeable fans (Tk1,500-3,000)
  • • Power banks for devices (Tk800-2,500)
  • • Battery-operated radios (Tk500-1,200)

Total investment: Tk3,000-7,000

Suitable for: Everyone, especially renters

Covers: 4-6 hours of basic lighting and device charging

2

Standard Tier: Inverter + Battery

The most popular option for Bangladesh homes:

Recommended Setups:

  • • Small: 850VA inverter + 100Ah battery (Tk14,000-18,000)
  • • Medium: 1500VA inverter + 150Ah battery (Tk24,000-30,000)
  • • Large: 2500VA inverter + 2x150Ah batteries (Tk40,000-50,000)

Popular brands: Rahimafrooz IPS, Energypac, Apollo

Suitable for: Most households and small offices

Covers: Lights, fans, TV, router, laptop (3-8 hours)

💡 Selection Tip: Choose inverter VA rating at least 1.5x your total connected load. For battery, each 100Ah provides roughly 1200 watt-hours of power.

3

Advanced Tier: Solar + Battery Storage

Sustainable solution with long-term savings:

Common Setups:

  • • Starter: 500W panels + 1kWh battery (Tk55,000-70,000)
  • • Standard: 1000W panels + 2kWh battery (Tk100,000-130,000)
  • • Comprehensive: 2000W panels + 5kWh battery (Tk200,000-250,000)

Available in Bangladesh: Solar Electro, Rahimafrooz Solar

Suitable for: Homeowners, long-term investment

Covers: 50-100% of household needs (depending on size)

💰 ROI Calculation: Most Bangladesh solar systems pay for themselves in 4-6 years through electricity savings and currently have government incentives reducing import duties.

4

Premium Tier: Generator Backup

For businesses and larger homes with critical needs:

Options Available:

  • • Portable petrol (1-3kW): Tk25,000-45,000
  • • Mid-size diesel (5-10kW): Tk80,000-150,000
  • • Automatic standby (10-20kW): Tk200,000-400,000

Reliable brands: Honda, Yamaha, Tiger, Lutian

Suitable for: Businesses, large homes, critical applications

Covers: Full power needs for extended periods

⚠️ Operating Cost: Generator running costs in Bangladesh average Tk50-80 per kWh compared to grid electricity at Tk7-12 per kWh. Use only for critical loads.

🕒 Pre-Load Shedding Preparation

30-Minute Preparation Routine

When you know a power cut is coming:

Immediate Actions:
  • ✓ Charge all devices to 100%
  • ✓ Fill water containers and storage tanks
  • ✓ Turn down refrigerator to coldest setting
  • ✓ Pre-cool rooms that need to stay comfortable
  • ✓ Check backup systems are ready and charged
  • ✓ Finish electric-dependent cooking
Communication & Safety:
  • ✓ Notify family members of expected outage time
  • ✓ Save and close important computer work
  • ✓ Position emergency lights in strategic locations
  • ✓ Unplug sensitive electronics
  • ✓ Place surge protectors on standby
  • ✓ Check for safety hazards (candles, etc.)

🔌 Power Restoration Safety

⚠️ Protecting Devices When Power Returns:

Voltage Fluctuation Protection:
  • • Use surge protectors on all valuable electronics
  • • Install voltage stabilizers for AC and refrigerator
  • • Wait 10-15 minutes before reconnecting sensitive devices
  • • Consider whole-house surge protection (Tk8,000-12,000)
Phase Balancing:
  • • Bangladesh power often returns with phase imbalance
  • • Measure voltage before reconnecting (normal: 210-240V)
  • • Use automatic voltage switchers where possible
  • • Most common damage occurs within 5 minutes of restoration

📱 Tech Solutions for Load Shedding

💻 Optimizing Work During Outages

Laptop Battery Extension:
  • • Reduce screen brightness to 50%
  • • Disable background apps and Bluetooth
  • • Use battery saver mode
  • • Close resource-heavy applications
  • • Consider an external laptop power bank
Internet Continuity:
  • • Set up mobile hotspot as backup
  • • Connect router to UPS/inverter
  • • Use 4G routers with battery backup
  • • Download important files before outage
  • • Enable offline mode for essential apps

💼 Business Continuity During Load Shedding

Business Load Shedding Strategy:

Critical Systems Protection:
  • • Dedicated UPS for servers and networking (minimum 30 minutes backup)
  • • Automated shutdown procedures for sensitive equipment
  • • Cloud backup solutions that sync during power availability
  • • Hybrid work policies allowing remote work during office outages
Cost-Effective Generator Management:
  • • Zone your business space (critical vs. non-critical areas)
  • • Run generator only 15 minutes per hour to charge devices/batteries
  • • Install load management system to prioritize circuits
  • • Consider diesel generator sharing among neighboring businesses
